Directions:

  1. Fill the bucket about a 1/3 full, it will be around 2 1/2 cups
  2. Lay down the tarp or whatever you choose to use
  3. Cut up a lot of strips of the gauze. Lengths should vary and be around 3"x1" and 2"x1"
  4. Generously cover hand in petroleum jelly"vaseline"
  5. Thoroughly wet the plaster gauze strips in water and start layering on the front side of the hand. You will want to create a clean seam
  6. Put on about 3 to 4 layers of the gauze on the front side
  7. You will then make what is called a flange. If you are wondering what a flange is, it is a raised edge created from built up gauze (refer to first image)
  8. Wait for mold to dry, it will get warm and then cool ( you will feel this on the outside of the mold, but refer to table if needed)
    9. Mold Thickness: Thin Layer(2-3) Average Layer(3-4) Thick Layer(5+)
    Dry Time: 10 Mins. 20-25 Mins. 50 Mins.
  9. Repeat 5-7 on the back side of the hand, after front side is dry. DO NOT overlap onto the front sides flange without putting petroleum jelly on it
  10. After both sides of dry its time to take the cast off. Have the owner of the hand gently move their fingers to loosen up the cast, then gently pull one side of the mold off the hand. This may take some time
  11. After getting the hand out of the mold put the two pieces together and layer some more gauze on the outside seam. Leave top open to pour what you choose to make the cast out of

There you have it you have made a mold of a hand!
